Step 6 · Scripts & Objection Handling

Close with warmth, not pressure.

Every script is MAS-compliant, empathetic, and tested. Tap a tab to switch between opening, objections, and the soft close.

Doorstep / First 60 seconds

"Hi Sarah, I'm Jason from Great Eastern — registered with MAS, Rep No. 1220629. Really happy to finally meet you! Congratulations again on the little one. Before I hand over the Supermom gift box, is now still a good time for a short 15-minute chat? If not, totally fine — we can reschedule."

Setting the frame (no-pressure)

"I want to be upfront — I'm not here to sell you anything today. My job is to understand where you are in this journey and share what other mums in your shoes have done. If anything resonates, we continue. If not, you still keep the gift box and my number for later. Fair?"

Transition to discovery

"Can I ask — is this your first baby? And are you more worried about the pregnancy itself, or about what happens after baby arrives?" (Let her talk. Listen. Don't interrupt.)

Open with empathy

"Every mum I meet has a different story. Some are most scared about gestational diabetes because their sister had it. Some are stressed about how to pay if baby needs NICU. Some haven't slept thinking about university fees. What's been on your mind lately?"

Probing gently

"When you say you're worried about [her top concern] — what does that look like for you day-to-day? Is it something you've thought about with your husband, or is it more on your heart alone?"

Anchoring on real stats

"Did you know 1 in 5 pregnancies in Singapore has some complication? And about 7% of babies are born with a congenital condition. These aren't scare stats — they're real. Which is why we built GREAT Maternity Care 2 specifically for mums like you."

Bridge to solution

"Based on what you've shared, I think there are two things worth looking at — protecting you and baby now during the pregnancy window, and starting a small education pot so you're not scrambling in 18 years. Shall I show you both?"

"I already have insurance from another company."
"That's great — you're already thinking ahead. Can I ask, is it an ILP with maternity rider, or a standalone maternity plan? Most SG insurers only offer it bundled inside a 20-year ILP. Great Eastern is the only one in Singapore offering standalone — one premium, done. Worth comparing side by side, no obligation."
"Let me discuss with my husband first."
"Absolutely, this is a family decision. Would it help if I prepared a one-page summary for you both to look at tonight? I can WhatsApp it over. Would tomorrow evening work for a quick follow-up — with him on the call if possible?"
"It sounds expensive right now — we're spending a lot on the baby."
"I hear you completely. A newborn already costs $10k in the first year. But here's the thing — the $5,000 Essential tier is a one-time $478. That's less than one month of infant care. And if something goes wrong medically, the payout is instant — no monthly premium burden during maternity leave. Would the Essential tier feel more manageable?"
"I'm healthy, I don't think I'll have complications."
"That's genuinely the best mindset. But let me share — 1 in 5 SG pregnancies do have a complication, and about 7% of babies have a congenital condition. Most of those mums were healthy too. This plan isn't for the worst case — it's so you don't worry during the good case. Makes sense?"
"Can I buy later, after baby is born?"
"Unfortunately, no — GMC2 can only be bought during pregnancy, from week 13 to before delivery. That's a hard cut-off. The Guaranteed Insurability Benefit is actually a big reason mums sign up — it lets you buy baby's future CI/hospital plan within 90 days of birth without medical underwriting, even if baby is born with a condition. That's a one-time window you don't get later."
"My husband already has coverage through his employer."
"That's good baseline protection. But two things worth knowing — employer coverage usually ends the day he leaves that job, and the payout is typically only 2–3× annual salary, which doesn't replace 20+ years of income needed to raise baby. A personal term life plan locks in coverage at today's age and health — and stays with him regardless of job changes. Shall I do a quick comparison?"
"Is Great Eastern reliable? What if the company collapses?"
"Great question. Great Eastern has been serving Singapore families since 1908 — over 117 years. We're part of OCBC Group. Plus, your policy is protected up to specified limits by SDIC — the Singapore government's insurance scheme. It's as safe as it gets."
"What if I have a miscarriage — does the money get wasted?"
"I'm sorry you even have to think about that. In that very situation — if it's a miscarriage due to a life-threatening condition — GMC2 actually pays you the full sum assured, because it's one of the 18 covered complications. It's built exactly for these heartbreaking moments."
"Why should I trust you specifically?"
"Fair question. I'm Jason Ng, Rep No. 1220629 — you can verify me on the MAS Register of Representatives. I've walked with hundreds of mums through this. You can check my Google reviews or speak to any of my existing clients. And everything I recommend is documented — you always have it in writing."
Soft assumptive close

"So based on what you've shared, what makes the most sense for your family is the Comprehensive $10,000 tier plus a small education savings plan starting at $246/month. The maternity is a one-time S$860. Shall I prepare the paperwork, or would you like to start with just the maternity piece today and we talk education next week?"

"Which tier feels right for you — the Essential at $478 one-time, or the Comprehensive at $860? There's no wrong answer, and Essential still covers everything we talked about, just at half the payout."
